The search for life beyond Earth has long fascinated scientists and space enthusiasts alike. Humans can then sharply narrow down the search for extraterrestrial life by searching only in places that have the conditions that Earth life requires: a source of energy, the presence of certain chemical compounds, and temperatures that allow liquid water to exist. One of the key prerequisites for life, as we know it, is the presence of liquid water. In 2024, space exploration made remarkable strides, discovering water on numerous celestial bodies and reinforcing the idea that liquid water may be more common than previously thought. When scientists and organizations search for extraterrestrial life, they aren't expecting to find advanced civilizations—rather, they focus on the possibility of discovering single-celled organisms, the fundamental building blocks of life.
